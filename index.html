Connecting with Students

We value feedback from students about our courses and associated resources. The Psychology Student Voice team consists of staff representatives and student representatives at all levels of study.
A key role of the student representatives is to work in partnership with the staff representatives and the student body to help enhance the student experience.

Student VoiceWhat is the Student Voice Committee

Student Reps

  • Student Voice Committee in the Department of Psychology is a committee made up of staff and students from Psychology and we work together to ensure the psychology courses are meeting the needs and expectations of our students and staff
  • We consider all the things that are going well on the courses, and we also look at possible improvements. The University relies on feedback from students to make sure that it is providing the best quality of education In the Department of Psychology, we are very keen to ensure the student voice is heard! A Student Rep's role is to be the ‘voice’ for your group

You said.... we didResponding to Level C Student feedback

Level C Core 1 2023/24

‘There are too many lectures focussing on the history of Psychology’

While you should appreciate the importance of the history of the discipline, we appreciate that feedback from students indicates it is not necessarily theior favourite topic. As such we have rewritten a lecture to give students more focussed direction about careers in psychology

Level C Core 1 2023/24

‘We think that more support is needed when using Jamovi.’

In response to this feedback we have added more face-to-face and online support to help students using Jamovi.

Level C Core 1 2023/24

‘The Moodle is confusing.’

The University undertook a feasibility study about the use of Moodle and it has has been redesigned to be more consistent across departments and easier for students to navigate and use.

Level C Core 1 2023/24

‘I enjoyed the interactive aspect of the lectures.’

It is very good news that students enjoyed the interactions in lectures and we want to thank you for engaging in them. These are the experiences that make for memorable lectures.

Level C Core 1 2023/24

‘We don’t like 9am lectures.’

We recognise that 9am lectures can be challenging for many, and we have explored ways to move 9am lectures with the University. At the moment the timetable cannot be moved.

Level C Core 2 2023/24

‘We enjoyed EDP sessions, particularly the method of assessment as it is quite different to anything else on the course’

We are delighted that you have enjoyed learning how to use PsychoPy and appreciate the innovative mode of assessment

Level C Core 2 2023/24

‘We would like to be able to practise for the lecture assignment’

We have increased the number of reflective exercises to encourage students to think how ethics can be applied to real-life situations. We hope that these will help you prepare for the assignment

Level C Core 2 2023/24

‘Reduce the amount of preparation material for each tutorial (reading and videos)’

We will have a review of the amount of preparation for this course element and we will communicate the outcome to students when complete

You said.... we didResponding to Level I Student feedback

Level I Core 1 2023/24

‘Introduce winter examinations so that we’re always assessed in a timely way.’

We are working with the University to explore ways in which the examinations could be organised to allow this to happen.We will keep students posted when we have more information.

Level I Core 1 2023/24

‘Make the lectures more interactive.’

Lecturers are being encouraged to provide more in-lecture interactive learning experiences. Educational technology is frequently used where it is felt that is appropriate for the content of the course. Don't forget that interactivity works both ways, so ask and answer questions in lectures for the best experience.

Level I Core 1 2023/24

‘We like the RDA video walkthroughs and use them a lot.’

This is great to hear. In response to this positive feedback we have actually increased the number of video walkthroughs for RDA to provide extra resources for statistical analyses.

Level I Core 1 2023/24

‘It would help if you could provide the opportunity to practice assessments.’

Example assessment questions have been incorporated into some lectures. Remember that feedback on your past assessments should also influence how you can work to improve your subsequent assessments as there are commonalities in all assessed work, referencing for example.

Level I Core 1 2023/24

‘Provide worksheets for RDA workshops.’

You complete worked examples in class and we have increased the number of video walkthroughs available. Based on student feedback we believe these are more dynamic than worksheets.

Level I Core 2 2023/24

‘Please add more introductory sessions to the Applied Psychology tutorials.’

We have added three new sessions at the beginning of the course to help ease students into the tutorials a bit more gently.

Level I Core 2 2023/24

‘We would appreciate more information on the Prevention Science seminar assessment.’

To support this assessment we have increased the information available describing the assessment. We have also decided to introduce feedback sessions during the seminars.

You said.... we didResponding to Level H Student feedback

Level H Core 1 2023/24

‘Can you provide definitions and key terms in the Psychobiology lectures.’

We will make sure to provide and explain all necessary definitions and key terms in the Psychobiology lectures. Ask questions if there is anything that you don't understand.

Level H Core 1 2023/24

‘We enjoyed the end of year poster conference.’

The dissertation poster conference is one of our yearly highlights as it is the culmination of your degree and we’re really pleased you enjoyed it. So did we!

Level H Core 1 2023/24

‘We found the MCT questions at the end of the Psychobiology lectures very helpful.’

Positive feedback is always welcome and we’re pleased to hear that you found the MCT questions helpful.

Level H Core 1 2023/24

‘The Poster conference was too close to the hand-in date for the dissertation’

We know tight deadlines cause stress and anxiety, especially around the submission for your dissertation. We have shifted the deadlines for the assessments to be slightly further apart to alleviate some of this added stress.

Level H Core 1 2023/24

‘The journal clubs in RDA3 were not helpful.’

This is somewhat disappointing to us. The purpose of journal clubs is to work through papers to model how studies should be written up in an academic style. We have re-structured and re-formatted RDA3, removing the journal clubs, but you will still be required to read and critique journal articles to support your dissertation.

Level H Core 2 2023/24

‘We found the assessment support session in the Cognitive Neuroscience option course useful, in particular how to structure an essay’

It is good to hear that changes we made to enhance this session to focus on essay structure and planning have been well received.

Level H Core 2 2023/24

‘Enjoyed the optional seminars, good range of options’

We have maintained the range of options available at Level H. These are subject to staffing, but we intend to keep providing a wide range of interesting options.

Level H Core 2 2023/24

‘In Psychology of Aesthetics and the Arts we liked the wide range of topics and the practical sessions’

Thank you for this feedback. Our goal is to have a balance of topics and practicals, so it's great that you value this.

Plas Caerdeon

18/10/23 Psychology staff members took to the hills with 30+ Psychology students to visit Plas Caerdeon, Hope's outdoor activity centre near Barmouth in Wales. Activities included kayaking, slate mine walking, zip wire and mountain biking to name just a few of the opportunities on offer.

Research Seminar

25/10/23 We are excited to announce our first departmental research seminar, starting next week on the 25th of October at 2pm in room SSA007. Dr. Irene Senna will be presenting her research on "Recovering from Blindness: Learning to see after sight restoration from congenital cataracts."
